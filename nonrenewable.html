WHAT ARE NONRENEWABLE RESOURCES

Nonrenewable energy resources include coal, natural gas, oil, and nuclear energy.
Once these resources are used up, they cannot be replaced, which is a major problem
for humanity as we are currently dependent on them to supply most of our energy needs
Nonrenewable resources do not exhibit significant growth or renewal over an economic
time scale. Examples would include coal, oil, and natural gas and minerals such as copper,
tin, iron, silver, and gold. We noted in Chapter 1 that a plant or animal species might
be more appropriately viewed as a nonrenewable resource than as a renewable resource.
In Chapter 2 we developed a model of a nonrenewable resource to show how Solver might
be used to determine the optimal time path of depletion.
